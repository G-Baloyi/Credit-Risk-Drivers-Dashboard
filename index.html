Study Conclusions
Analytical Conclusion
"Credit risk is not a single-variable problem. Default likelihood is shaped by the convergence of loan structure, demographic context, and behavioral financial patterns — institutions that monitor all three dimensions are better positioned to reduce exposure and allocate capital effectively."
01
Loan Structure Drives Baseline Risk
Loan amount, duration, and credit intensity are the strongest structural predictors. High-intensity, long-duration loans represent the highest-risk segment.
02
Cash Flow Is the Leading Behavioral Indicator
Chronically negative net cash flow correlates strongly with high-risk classification — monitoring trends enables earlier detection.
03
Behavioral Analytics Improve Segmentation
Transaction volatility and spending ratios add predictive power beyond traditional metrics, reducing misclassification in the medium-risk band.
