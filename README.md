# Credit Risk Drivers Dashboard

## Overview

This dashboard analyzes the key factors influencing customer credit risk using demographic, financial, and loan-related features. The goal is to identify patterns that distinguish high-risk from low-risk customers and support data-driven lending decisions.

---

## Dataset

The analysis combines:

* Customer demographic and credit data
* Transaction behavior and financial activity

Key variables include:

* Age, Job, Housing Status
* Credit Amount, Loan Duration
* Transaction behavior metrics (spending, cash flow, volatility)

---

## Feature Engineering

The following calculated fields were created to enhance analysis:

* **Net Cash Flow** = Total Received – Total Spent
* **Transaction Volatility** = Variation in transaction behavior
* **Credit Intensity** = Loan burden relative to customer profile
* **Customer Risk Score** = Composite indicator of risk
* **Risk Flag** = High-risk classification indicator

---

## Dashboard Components

The dashboard includes six key visualizations:

1. **Risk Distribution Overview**
   Displays the proportion of high-risk vs low-risk customers.

2. **Risk Distribution by Age Group**
   Highlights how credit risk varies across age segments.

3. **Credit Amount vs Loan Duration**
   Shows the relationship between loan size, duration, and risk.

4. **Credit Risk by Job Category**
   Examines how employment level impacts risk.

5. **Credit Risk by Housing Status**
   Compares risk across ownership vs rental status.

6. **Loan Intensity vs Risk**
   Analyzes how financial burden relates to credit risk.

---

## Key Insights

* Younger customers exhibit higher credit risk due to lower financial stability
* Higher loan amounts and longer durations are associated with increased risk
* Customers in lower job categories show greater default likelihood
* Homeownership is strongly linked to lower credit risk
* High credit intensity is a key indicator of financial stress

---

## Business Impact

These insights can support:

* Improved credit risk assessment models
* Better customer segmentation
* More informed loan approval decisions
* Targeted risk mitigation strategies
